# Legalese to Human Glossary

A quick reference to translate common legal terms into plain English.

| Term | Legalese Logic | Plain Human English |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Arbitration** | A private dispute resolution process. | A private court that usually favors the company. You lose the right to a jury. |
| **Indemnification** | To compensate for loss or damage. | You promise to pay for the company’s mistakes if they get sued. |
| **Liability** | Responsibility for an action or event. | Who has to pay if something goes wrong. |
| **Choice of Law** | The law that governs the contract. | The set of rules that will be used to judge a dispute (e.g., California law vs. New York law). |
| **Severability** | If one part is invalid, the rest stays. | If a judge finds one clause illegal, the rest of the contract is still active. |
| **Jurisdiction** | The physical location for disputes. | The place where you have to travel if you want to sue or defend yourself. |
| **Termination for Convenience** | Ending the contract at any time. | The company can fire you as a customer whenever they want for no reason. |
| **Negative Option** | Passive acceptance of terms or renewals. | "Silence means yes." If you don't say no, they keep charging you. |
| **Force Majeure** | Unforeseeable "Acts of God". | Unpredictable disasters (war, floods, pandemics) that excuse the company from doing their job. |
| **Survival Clause** | Terms that last after the contract ends. | Rules that still apply to you even after you stop using the service. |
| **Waiver** | Intentional surrender of a right. | You are giving up a legal protection you would normally have. |
